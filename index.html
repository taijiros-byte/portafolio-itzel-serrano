Introduction

This digital portfolio showcases the lessons studied during the semester, integrating grammar, vocabulary, and interactive activities designed to improve English proficiency. Each section contains summaries and personal reflections, aiming to present the learning outcomes and their relevance for the future.

Lesson 1: Introducing Yourself

Objective: Practice self‑introductions using simple sentences.

We learned to talk about ourselves, including name, age, birthday, favorite subjects, and hobbies.

Reflection: This helped me gain confidence when speaking about myself. I now know how to describe who I am.

Lesson 2: Technology Uses

Objective: Write affirmative and negative sentences using simple present.

We practiced how to talk about daily technology habits using present tense.

Reflection: I improved writing structured sentences and expressing habits clearly.

Lesson 3: Parts of the Computer

Objective: Identify basic hardware parts of a computer and their functions.

We labeled the monitor, CPU, mouse, keyboard, printer, hard drive, and CD drive.

Reflection: I can now describe each part of the computer and explain its purpose.

Lesson 4: Action Verbs in Computing

Objective: Use action verbs related to computing in sentences.

We learned verbs like click, drag, install, run, update, type, etc.

Reflection: I can give and follow technical instructions more clearly now.

Lesson 5: Professional Email

Objective: Learn the structure of a formal email and appropriate expressions.

We wrote emails using greetings, body paragraphs, and formal closings.

Reflection: I now know how to write professional emails with correct formatting and tone.

Lesson 6: Internet Safety

Objective: Understand online threats and learn how to stay safe online.

We discussed risks such as phishing and created online safety posters.

Reflection: I learned how to protect my personal data and behave responsibly on the internet.

Lesson 7: Types of Computers

Objective: Compare desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones.

We described the function and portability of each device type.

Reflection: I can identify which computer type suits different needs and situations.

Lesson 8: Computers in the Past

Objective: Discuss how computers have evolved over time.

We explored the history of computing and major milestones.

Reflection: I appreciate how technology has progressed and made life easier.

Lesson 9: Questions in Past Tense

Objective: Form and answer WH‑ questions in past tense.

We practiced questions like 'What did you do yesterday?'

Reflection: Now I can ask and answer about past events clearly.

Lesson 10: The Future and Robotics

Objective: Explore how robotics may impact future life.

We discussed benefits and challenges of using robots.

Reflection: I learned that robots will help in medicine, education, and daily tasks.

Lesson 11: Environmental Solutions

Objective: Propose solutions to environmental problems.

We talked about recycling, pollution, and saving energy.

Reflection: I now value small actions to protect the planet.

Lesson 12: Reflection and Review

Objective: Review course contents and share reflections.

We reviewed vocabulary, grammar, and all previous topics.

Reflection: This helped me connect all lessons and recognize my progress.

Conclusion

Throughout this course, I have strengthened my English communication skills and broadened my understanding of technology and its impact. This portfolio reflects my journey and growth over the semester.
